2b:T147b1,<h1 id="the-beta-distribution">The beta distribution<a class="anchor" aria-hidden="true" hidden href="#the-beta-distribution">#</a></h1>
<p>The beta distribution is a powerful tool for modeling probabilities and proportions between 0 and 1. Here's a structured intuition to grasp its essence:</p>
<h3 id="core-concept">Core Concept<a class="anchor" aria-hidden="true" hidden href="#core-concept">#</a></h3>
<p>The beta distribution, defined on <span class="katex"><span class="katex-html" aria-hidden="true"><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height:1em;vertical-align:-0.25em;"></span><span class="mopen">[</span><span class="mord">0</span><span class="mpunct">,</span><span class="mspace" style="margin-right:0.1667em;"></span><span class="mord">1</span><span class="mclose">]</span></span></span></span>, is parameterized by two shape parameters: <strong>α (alpha)</strong> and <strong>β (beta)</strong>. These parameters dictate the distribution’s shape, allowing it to flexibly represent beliefs about probabilities, rates, or proportions.</p>
<h3 id="key-intuitions">Key Intuitions<a class="anchor" aria-hidden="true" hidden href="#key-intuitions">#</a></h3>
<h4 id="a-pseudo-counts-interpretation"><strong>a. "Pseudo-Counts" Interpretation</strong><a class="anchor" aria-hidden="true" hidden href="#a-pseudo-counts-interpretation">#</a></h4>
<ul>
<li><strong>α</strong> acts like "successes" and <strong>β</strong> like "failures" in a hypothetical experiment.
<ul>
<li>Example: If you use <strong>Beta(5, 3)</strong>, it’s as if you’ve observed <strong>5 successes</strong> and <strong>3 failures</strong> <em>before seeing actual data</em>.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>After observing <strong>x real successes</strong> and <strong>y real failures</strong>, the posterior becomes <strong>Beta(α+x, β+y)</strong>. This makes beta the <strong>conjugate prior</strong> for the binomial distribution (bernoulli process).</li>
</ul>
<h4 id="b-shape-flexibility"><strong>b. Shape Flexibility</strong><a class="anchor" aria-hidden="true" hidden href="#b-shape-flexibility">#</a></h4>
<ul>
<li><strong>Uniform distribution</strong>: When <strong>α = β = 1</strong>, all values in [0, 1] are equally likely.</li>
<li><strong>Bell-shaped</strong>: When <strong>α, β > 1</strong>, the distribution peaks at <strong>mode = (α-1)/(α+β-2)</strong>.
<ul>
<li>Symmetric if <strong>α = β</strong> (e.g., <strong>Beta(5, 5)</strong> is centered at 0.5).</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><strong>U-shaped</strong>: When <strong>α, β &#x3C; 1</strong>, density spikes at 0 and 1 (useful for modeling polarization, meaning we believe the model to only produce values at 0 or 1, not in the middle.).</li>
<li><strong>Skewed</strong>: If <strong>α > β</strong>, skewed toward 1; if <strong>β > α</strong>, skewed toward 0.</li>
</ul>
<h4 id="c-moments"><strong>c. Moments</strong><a class="anchor" aria-hidden="true" hidden href="#c-moments">#</a></h4>
<ul>
<li><strong>Mean</strong>: <span class="katex"><span class="katex-html" aria-hidden="true"><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height:1em;vertical-align:-0.25em;"></span><span class="mord mathnormal" style="margin-right:0.0037em;">α</span><span class="mord">/</span><span class="mopen">(</span><span class="mord mathnormal" style="margin-right:0.0037em;">α</span><span class="mspace" style="margin-right:0.2222em;"></span><span class="mbin">+</span><span class="mspace" style="margin-right:0.2222em;"></span></span><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height:1em;vertical-align:-0.25em;"></span><span class="mord mathnormal" style="margin-right:0.0528em;">β</span><span class="mclose">)</span></span></span></span> – your "expected" probability of success.</li>
<li><strong>Variance</strong>: <span class="katex"><span class="katex-html" aria-hidden="true"><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height:1em;vertical-align:-0.25em;"></span><span class="mord mathnormal" style="margin-right:0.0037em;">α</span><span class="mord mathnormal" style="margin-right:0.0528em;">β</span><span class="mord">/</span><span class="mopen">[(</span><span class="mord mathnormal" style="margin-right:0.0037em;">α</span><span class="mspace" style="margin-right:0.2222em;"></span><span class="mbin">+</span><span class="mspace" style="margin-right:0.2222em;"></span></span><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height:1.0641em;vertical-align:-0.25em;"></span><span class="mord mathnormal" style="margin-right:0.0528em;">β</span><span class="mclose"><span class="mclose">)</span><span class="msupsub"><span class="vlist-t"><span class="vlist-r"><span class="vlist" style="height:0.8141em;"><span style="top:-3.063em;margin-right:0.05em;"><span class="pstrut" style="height:2.7em;"></span><span class="sizing reset-size6 size3 mtight"><span class="mord mtight"><span class="mord mtight">2</span></span></span></span></span></span></span></span></span><span class="mopen">(</span><span class="mord mathnormal" style="margin-right:0.0037em;">α</span><span </ul>
<p>One can also compute the mode and discover it is:</p>
<span class="katex-display"><span class="katex"><span class="katex-html" aria-hidden="true"><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height:0.6944em;"></span><span class="mord text"><span class="mord">Mode</span></span><span class="mspace" style="margin-right:0.2778em;"></span><span class="mrel">=</span><span class="mspace" style="margin-right:0.2778em;"></span></span><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height:2.2019em;vertical-align:-0.8804em;"></span><span class="mord"><span class="mopen nulldelimiter"></span><span class="mfrac"><span class="vlist-t vlist-t2"><span class="vlist-r"><span class="vlist" style="height:1.3214em;"><span style="top:-2.314em;"><span class="pstrut" style="height:3em;"></span><span class="mord"><span class="mord mathnormal" style="margin-right:0.0037em;">α</span><span class="mspace" style="margin-right:0.2222em;"></span><span class="mbin">+</span><span class="mspace" style="margin-right:0.2222em;"></span><span class="mord mathnormal" style="margin-right:0.0528em;">β</span><span class="mspace" style="margin-right:0.2222em;"></span><span class="mbin">−</span><span class="mspace" style="margin-right:0.2222em;"></span><span class="mord">2</span></span></span><span style="top:-3.23em;"><span class="pstrut" style="height:3em;"></span><span class="frac-line" style="border-bottom-width:0.04em;"></span></span><span style="top:-3.677em;"><span class="pstrut" style="height:3em;"></span><span class="mord"><span class="mord mathnormal" style="margin-right:0.0037em;">α</span><span class="mspace" style="margin-right:0.2222em;"></span><span class="mbin">−</span><span class="mspace" style="margin-right:0.2222em;"></span><span class="mord">1</span></span></span></span><span class="vlist-s">​</span></span><span class="vlist-r"><span class="vlist" style="height:0.8804em;"><span></span></span></span></span></span><span class="mclose nulldelimiter"></span></span></span></span></span></span>
<h3 id="the-mathematical-model">The mathematical model<a class="anchor" aria-hidden="true" hidden href="#the-mathematical-model">#</a></h3>
<p>The beta distribution is known as:</p>

<h3 id="visualization-parameter-tuning">Visualization &#x26; Parameter Tuning<a class="anchor" aria-hidden="true" hidden href="#visualization-parameter-tuning">#</a></h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Mean and Variance Control</strong>:<br>
To design a beta distribution with mean <strong>μ</strong> and variance <strong>σ²</strong>, solve:
<ul>
<li><strong>α = μ(μ(1−μ)/σ² − 1)</strong></li>
<li><strong>β = (1−μ)(μ(1−μ)/σ² − 1)</strong><br>
Example: For <strong>μ = 0.5</strong>, <strong>σ² = 0.01</strong>, use <strong>Beta(12, 12)</strong>.</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
