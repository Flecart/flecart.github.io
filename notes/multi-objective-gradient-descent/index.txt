22:T11284,<h2 id="multi-objective-gradient-descent-mgda">Multi-Objective Gradient Descent (MGDA)<a class="anchor" aria-hidden="true" hidden href="#multi-objective-gradient-descent-mgda">#</a></h2>
<h3 id="problem-formulation">Problem Formulation<a class="anchor" aria-hidden="true" hidden href="#problem-formulation">#</a></h3>
<p>Brief context: standard gradient descent optimizes a single scalar loss. MGDA generalizes this to <span class="katex"><span class="katex-html" aria-hidden="true"><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height:0.6833em;"></span><span class="mord mathnormal" style="margin-right:0.1389em;">T</span></span></span></span> tasks, seeking <strong>Pareto-optimal</strong> solutions rather than a single weighted compromise.</p>
<h4 id="multi-objective-optimization-setup">Multi-Objective Optimization Setup<a class="anchor" aria-hidden="true" hidden href="#multi-objective-optimization-setup">#</a></h4>
<p><strong>Single-objective baseline</strong>: minimize <span class="katex"><span class="katex-html" aria-hidden="true"><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height:1em;vertical-align:-0.25em;"></span><span class="mord mathcal">L</span><span class="mopen">(</span><span class="mord mathnormal" style="margin-right:0.0278em;">θ</span><span class="mclose">)</span></span></span></span> — one gradient, one direction.</p>
<p><strong>Multi-objective generalization</strong>: given <span class="katex"><span class="katex-html" aria-hidden="true"><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height:0.6833em;"></span><span class="vlist-r"><span class="vlist" style="height:0.2806em;"><span style="top:-2.55em;margin-left:0em;margin-right:0.05em;"><span class="pstrut" style="height:2.7em;"></span><span class="sizing reset-size6 size3 mtight"><span class="mord mathnormal mtight">t</span></span></span></span><span class="vlist-s">​</span></span><span class="vlist-r"><span class="vlist" class="mclose">)</span><span class="mclose"><span class="mclose">}</span><span class="msupsub"><span class="vlist-t vlist-t2"><span class="vlist-r"><span class="vlist" style="height:0.8413em;"><span </blockquote>
<p><strong>Pareto stationarity</strong> (weaker, necessary condition): <span class="katex"><span class="katex-html" aria-hidden="true"><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height:0.6944em;"></span><span class="mord mathnormal" style="margin-right:0.0278em;">θ</span></span></span></span> is Pareto stationary if no infinitesimal move simultaneously decreases all losses. MGDA converges to this.</p>
<blockquote>
<p>[!note] Pareto Stationary ≠ Pareto Optimal
Pareto stationarity is only a necessary condition (like critical points in single-objective). In nonconvex settings, MGDA may converge to saddle points on the front.</p>
</blockquote>

<blockquote>
<p>[!note] PCGrad vs MGDA
PCGrad (Gradient Surgery) projects each task's gradient onto the normal plane of conflicting gradients. Cheaper and often competitive empirically, but theoretically weaker — no guarantee of common descent.</p>
</blockquote>
<h3 id="practical-considerations--drawbacks">Practical Considerations &#x26; Drawbacks<a class="anchor" aria-hidden="true" hidden href="#practical-considerations--drawbacks">#</a></h3>
<h4 id="limitations-and-failure-modes">Limitations and Failure Modes<a class="anchor" aria-hidden="true" hidden href="#limitations-and-failure-modes">#</a></h4>
<ul>
<li><strong>Large <span class="katex"><span class="katex-html" aria-hidden="true"><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height:0.6833em;"></span><span class="mord mathnormal" style="margin-right:0.1389em;">T</span></span></span></span></strong>: QP cost grows as <span class="katex"><span class="katex-html" aria-hidden="true"><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height:1.0641em;vertical-align:-0.25em;"></span><span class="mord mathnormal" style="margin-right:0.0278em;">O</span><span class="mopen">(</span><span class="mord"><span class="mord mathnormal" style="margin-right:0.1389em;">T</span><span class="msupsub"><span class="vlist-t"><span class="vlist-r"><span class="vlist" style="height:0.8141em;"><span style="top:-3.063em;margin-right:0.05em;"><span class="pstrut" style="height:2.7em;"></span><span class="sizing reset-size6 size3 mtight"><span class="mord mtight">2</span></span></span></span></span></span></span></span><span class="mclose">)</span></span></span></span> in gradient dimensionality; for <span class="katex"><span class="katex-html" aria-hidden="true"><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height:0.7224em;vertical-align:-0.0391em;"></span><span class="mord mathnormal" style="margin-right:0.1389em;">T</span><span class="mspace" style="margin-right:0.2778em;"></span><span class="mrel">></span><span class="mspace" style="margin-right:0.2778em;"></span></span><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height:0.6444em;"></span><span class="mord">50</span></span></span></span> tasks, this is prohibitive. Mitigations: random task subsampling, approximate QP solvers.</li>
<li><strong>Gradient noise</strong>: stochastic <span class="katex"><span class="katex-html" aria-hidden="true"><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height:0.625em;vertical-align:-0.1944em;"></span><span class="mord"><span class="mord mathnormal" style="margin-right:0.0359em;">g</span><span class="msupsub"><span class="vlist-t vlist-t2"><span class="vlist-r"><span class="vlist" style="height:0.2806em;"><span style="top:-2.55em;margin-left:-0.0359em;margin-right:0.05em;"><span class="pstrut" style="height:2.7em;"></span><span class="sizing reset-size6 size3 mtight"><span class="mord mathnormal mtight">t</span></span></span></span><span class="vlist-s">​</span></span><span class="vlist-r"><span class="vlist" style="height:0.15em;"><span></span></span></span></span></span></span></span></span></span> inflates <span class="katex"><span class="katex-html" aria-hidden="true"><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height:0.6833em;"></span><span class="mord mathnormal">G</span></span></span></span> — the solver may spuriously detect "conflict" where none exists. Larger batches or gradient averaging help.</li>
<li><strong>No front exploration</strong>: converges to a single point; must re-run with different seeds/initializations to map the Pareto front.</li>
<li><strong>Non-convex front</strong>: MGDA finds a Pareto <em>critical</em> point, not necessarily Pareto <em>optimal</em>. In practice, one cannot verify optimality without exhaustive search.</li>
<li><strong>Representation bottleneck (MGDA-UB)</strong>: if the task heads are large or the encoder small, the upper bound is loose and the approximation degrades.</li>
</ul>
<blockquote>
<p>[!warning] Adam + MGDA Interaction
Naively applying MGDA after Adam preconditioning changes the geometry — Adam's diagonal preconditioning distorts inner products in <span class="katex"><span class="katex-html" aria-hidden="true"><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height:0.6833em;"></span><span class="mord mathnormal">G</span></span></span></span>. Either use raw gradients for <span class="katex"><span class="katex-html" aria-hidden="true"><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height:0.6833em;"></span><span class="mord mathnormal">G</span></span></span></span> and Adam-preconditioned for the update, or use a consistent metric throughout.</p>
</blockquote>
