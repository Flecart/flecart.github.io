Intuition#

The most important observation that allows Fourier series approximation is that given k=1,2,… we have that

2π​1​,π​cos(kx)​,π​sin(kx)​,…

∫02π​(sin(kx))2dx=∫02π​(cos(kx))2dx=π ∫02π​sin(kx)sin(hx)dx=∫02π​cos(kx)cos(hx)dx=0

∫02π​sin(kx)cos(hx)dx=∫02π​sin(kx)dx=∫02π​cos(hx)dx=0

In this section we quickly prove why the above equations hold. First we all agree that ∫02π​sin(kx)dx=∫02π​cos(hx)dx=0 because their period divides 2π and the sum of the area of a period is clearly 0. Or we can explicitly find the primitive and solve

For the other relations we need to remember some trigonometric identities:

sin(kx)cos(hx)=21​[sin(kx+hx)+sin(kx−hx)] And if we solve now the integral we can see that

∫02π​sin(kx)cos(hx)dx=21​[∫02π​sin((k+h)x)d+∫02π​sin((k−h)x)dx] ==21​[k+h−1​cos((k+h)x)​02π​+k−h−1​cos((k−h)x)​02π​]=21​(0+0)

And then you can prove every relation.
