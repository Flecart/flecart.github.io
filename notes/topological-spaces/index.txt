<h4 id="def-interior-and-boundaries">Def: Interior and boundaries<a class="anchor" aria-hidden="true" hidden href="#def-interior-and-boundaries">#</a></h4>
<p>Given a topology <span class="katex"><span class="katex-html" aria-hidden="true"><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height:1em;vertical-align:-0.25em;"></span><span class="mopen">(</span><span class="mord mathnormal" style="margin-right:0.0785em;">X</span><span class="mpunct">,</span><span class="mspace" style="margin-right:0.1667em;"></span><span class="mord mathcal" style="margin-right:0.2542em;">T</span><span class="mclose">)</span></span></span></span> we call a set <span class="katex"><span class="katex-html" aria-hidden="true"><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height:0.8193em;vertical-align:-0.136em;"></span><span class="mord mathnormal" style="margin-right:0.0576em;">S</span><span class="mspace" style="margin-right:0.2778em;"></span><span class="mrel">⊆</span><span class="mspace" style="margin-right:0.2778em;"></span></span><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height:0.6833em;"></span><span class="mord mathnormal" style="margin-right:0.0785em;">X</span></span></span></span> open if <span class="katex"><span class="katex-html" aria-hidden="true"><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height:0.7224em;vertical-align:-0.0391em;"></span><span class="mord mathnormal" style="margin-right:0.0576em;">S</span><span class="mspace" style="margin-right:0.2778em;"></span><span class="mrel">∈</span><span class="mspace" style="margin-right:0.2778em;"></span></span><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height:0.6833em;"></span><span class="mord mathcal" style="margin-right:0.2542em;">T</span></span></span></span>. This is a easier definition of open and closeness compared to the definition present in real analysis. In this case the concept of neighbourhood is built in.</p>
<p>We cal <strong>interior set</strong> of <span class="katex"><span class="katex-html" aria-hidden="true"><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height:0.6833em;"></span><span class="mord mathnormal">A</span></span></span></span> the union of all open sets of <span class="katex"><span class="katex-html" aria-hidden="true"><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height:0.6833em;"></span><span class="mord mathnormal">A</span></span></span></span>. <li>Continuous</li>
<li>Inverse is continuous

<h4 id="def-surface">Def: Surface<a class="anchor" aria-hidden="true" hidden href="#def-surface">#</a></h4>
<blockquote>
<p>A surface is a topological space in which each point has a neighbourhood homeomorphic to the plane, and for which any two distinct points possess disjoint neighborhoods.</p>
</blockquote>
<h3 id="classification-theorem">Classification Theorem<a class="anchor" aria-hidden="true" hidden href="#classification-theorem">#</a></h3>
<blockquote>
<p>Any closed surface is homeomorphic either to the sphere, or to the sphere with a finite number of handles added, or to the sphere with a finite number of discs removed and replaced by Möbius strips. No two of these surfaces are homeomorphic.</p>
</blockquote>
<p>This is one of the most important theorems in topology, but for a student that just started to understand this field, it will be probably a little difficult to do so.</p>
