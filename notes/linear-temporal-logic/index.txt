2f:T1cdae,<h2 id="linear-temporal-logic-ltl">Linear Temporal Logic (LTL)<a class="anchor" aria-hidden="true" hidden href="#linear-temporal-logic-ltl">#</a></h2>
<h3 id="motivation-historical-context">Motivation &#x26; Historical Context<a class="anchor" aria-hidden="true" hidden href="#motivation-historical-context">#</a></h3>
<p>Temporal logic was introduced into computer science by <strong>Amir Pnueli (1977)</strong> in his seminal paper <em>"The Temporal Logic of Programs"</em>, for which he later received the Turing Award. The driving idea: classical logic asserts truths about a static world, but programs, especially reactive, non-terminating systems, evolve over time. We need a logic whose truth values are indexed by moments in an execution.</p>
<p>Two foundational shifts mark its arrival:</p>
<ol>
<li><strong>From state assertions to trace assertions</strong>: Floyd-Hoare logic reasons about pre/post-conditions of terminating programs; LTL reasons about <em>infinite</em> behaviors of ongoing systems (operating systems, controllers, protocols, agents).</li>
<li><strong>From branching to linear time</strong> (a deliberate design choice): a single execution trace is the semantic object; branching is captured by Computation Tree Logic (CTL) instead.</li>
</ol>
<p>This matters directly to your work: any multi-agent system needs a specification language. LTL is the <strong>lingua franca</strong> for safety and liveness of distributed/reactive systems and underlies modern model checkers (SPIN, NuSMV, PRISM) as well as runtime monitoring for AI agents and contractual specifications.</p>

<h3 id="expressiveness">Expressiveness<a class="anchor" aria-hidden="true" hidden href="#expressiveness">#</a></h3>
<h4 id="kamps-theorem">Kamp's Theorem<a class="anchor" aria-hidden="true" hidden href="#kamps-theorem">#</a></h4>
<blockquote>
<p><strong>Kamp (1968)</strong>: LTL with <span class="katex"><span class="katex-html" aria-hidden="true"><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height:0.6861em;"></span><span class="mord mathbf">U</span></span></span></span> and its past-time dual is expressively equivalent to <strong>first-order logic over</strong> <span class="katex"><span class="katex-html" aria-hidden="true"><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height:1em;vertical-align:-0.25em;"></span><span class="mopen">(</span><span class="mord mathbb">N</span><span class="mpunct">,</span><span class="mspace" style="margin-right:0.2778em;"></span><span class="mrel">&#x3C;</span></span><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height:1em;vertical-align:-0.25em;"></span><span class="mclose">)</span></span></span></span> on traces.</p>
</blockquote>
<p>That is, LTL = <span class="katex"><span class="katex-html" aria-hidden="true"><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height:1em;vertical-align:-0.25em;"></span><span class="mord mathnormal" style="margin-right:0.1389em;">F</span><span class="mord mathnormal" style="margin-right:0.0278em;">O</span><span class="mopen">[</span><span class="mrel">&#x3C;</span></span><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height:1em;vertical-align:-0.25em;"></span><span class="mclose">]</span></span></span></span> on linear orders. Equivalently, LTL captures exactly the <strong>star-free</strong> <span class="katex"><span class="katex-html" aria-hidden="true"><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height:0.4306em;"></span><span class="mord mathnormal" style="margin-right:0.0359em;">ω</span></span></span></span>-regular languages.</p>
<div class="callout" data-callout="note">
<div class="callout-title"><span class="callout-icon" aria-hidden="true"><svg xmlns="http://www.w3.org/2000/svg" viewBox="0 0 24 24" width="18" height="18" fill="none" stroke="currentColor" stroke-width="2" stroke-linecap="round" stroke-linejoin="round"><path d="M12 20h9"></path><path d="M16.5 3.5a2.12 2.12 0 0 1 3 3L7 19l-4 1 1-4Z"></path></svg></span><span class="callout-title-inner">Why this is deep</span></div>
<div class="callout-content"><p>The full class of <span class="katex"><span class="katex-html" aria-hidden="true"><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height:0.4306em;"></span><span class="mord mathnormal" style="margin-right:0.0359em;">ω</span></span></span></span>-regular languages is captured by Monadic Second-Order Logic (MSO) (Büchi's theorem). LTL is strictly weaker: it cannot express "<span class="katex"><span class="katex-html" aria-hidden="true"><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height:0.625em;vertical-align:-0.1944em;"></span><span class="mord mathnormal">p</span></span></span></span> holds at all even positions" (this is regular but not star-free). To match <span class="katex"><span class="katex-html" aria-hidden="true"><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height:0.4306em;"></span><span class="mord mathnormal" style="margin-right:0.0359em;">ω</span></span></span></span>-regular expressiveness you need <span class="katex"><span class="katex-html" aria-hidden="true"><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height:0.625em;vertical-align:-0.1944em;"></span><span class="mord mathnormal">μ</span></span></span></span>-calculus, QPTL (with proposition quantifiers), or ETL with regular grammar operators.</p></div>
</div>

<h3 id="past-time-ltl-variants">Past-Time LTL &#x26; Variants<a class="anchor" aria-hidden="true" hidden href="#past-time-ltl-variants">#</a></h3>
<h4 id="past-operators">Past Operators<a class="anchor" aria-hidden="true" hidden href="#past-operators">#</a></h4>
<p>Past LTL (PLTL) adds:</p>
<ul>
<li><span class="katex"><span class="katex-html" aria-hidden="true"><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height:0.8805em;vertical-align:-0.1944em;"></span><span class="mord mathbf" style="margin-right:0.0288em;">Y</span><span class="mord mathnormal">φ</span></span></span></span> ,  <em>yesterday</em> (previous step; false at position 0)</li>
<li><span class="katex"><span class="katex-html" aria-hidden="true"><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height:0.8805em;vertical-align:-0.1944em;"></span><span class="mord mathbf">O</span><span class="mord mathnormal">φ</span></span></span></span> ,  <em>once</em> (some past)</li>
<li><span class="katex"><span class="katex-html" aria-hidden="true"><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height:0.8805em;vertical-align:-0.1944em;"></span><span class="mord mathbf">H</span><span class="mord mathnormal">φ</span></span></span></span> ,  <em>historically</em> (always past)</li>
<li><span class="katex"><span class="katex-html" aria-hidden="true"><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height:0.8889em;vertical-align:-0.1944em;"></span><span class="mord mathnormal">φ</span><span class="mord mathbf">S</span><span class="mord mathnormal" style="margin-right:0.0359em;">ψ</span></span></span></span> ,  <em>since</em></li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Expressively</strong> PLTL = LTL (Gabbay's separation theorem allows pure-past + pure-future decomposition), so no new <span class="katex"><span class="katex-html" aria-hidden="true"><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height:0.4306em;"></span><span class="mord mathnormal" style="margin-right:0.0359em;">ω</span></span></span></span>-languages. But <strong>succinctness</strong>: past operators can yield exponentially shorter formulas. Specs like "any acknowledgement was preceded by a request" become trivial: <span class="katex"><span class="katex-html" aria-hidden="true"><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height:1em;vertical-align:-0.25em;"></span><span class="mord mathbf">G</span><span class="mopen">(</span><span class="mord text"><span class="mord">ack</span></span><span class="mspace" style="margin-right:0.2778em;"></span><span class="mrel">→</span><span class="mspace" style="margin-right:0.2778em;"></span></span><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height:1em;vertical-align:-0.25em;"></span><span class="mord mathbf">O</span><span class="mspace" style="margin-right:0.1667em;"></span><span class="mord text"><span class="mord">req</span></span><span class="mclose">)</span></span></span></span>. For runtime verification of AI agents, past operators are natural since monitors observe history.</p>
<h4 id="real-time-/-metric-ltl">Real-Time / Metric LTL<a class="anchor" aria-hidden="true" hidden href="#real-time-/-metric-ltl">#</a></h4>
<p>Metric Temporal Logic (MTL) and <strong>Timed LTL</strong> add quantitative bounds: <span class="katex"><span class="katex-html" aria-hidden="true"><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height:1.0413em;vertical-align:-0.3552em;"></span><span class="mord"><span class="mord mathbf">F</span><span class="msupsub"><span class="vlist-t vlist-t2"><span class="vlist-r"><span class="vlist" style="height:0.3448em;"><span style="top:-2.5198em;margin-left:0em;margin-right:0.05em;"><span class="pstrut" style="height:2.7em;"></span><span class="sizing reset-size6 size3 mtight"><span class="mord mtight"><span class="mopen mtight">[</span><span class="mord mtight">0</span><span class="mpunct mtight">,</span><span class="mord mtight">5</span><span class="mclose mtight">]</span></span></span></span></span><span class="vlist-s">​</span></span><span class="vlist-r"><span class="vlist" style="height:0.3552em;"><span></span></span></span></span></span></span><span class="mord mathnormal">φ</span></span></span></span>. <h4 id="probabilistic-quantitative-variants">Probabilistic &#x26; Quantitative Variants<a class="anchor" aria-hidden="true" hidden href="#probabilistic-quantitative-variants">#</a></h4>
<ul>
<li><strong>PCTL</strong> / <strong>PLTL</strong> (probabilistic LTL) ,  interpret over Markov Decision Process / DTMC; replace <span class="katex"><span class="katex-html" aria-hidden="true"><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height:0.6861em;"></span><span class="mord mathbf">A</span></span></span></span> with <span class="katex"><span class="katex-html" aria-hidden="true"><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height:0.975em;vertical-align:-0.2861em;"></span><span class="mord"><span class="mord mathbb">P</span><span class="msupsub"><span class="vlist-t vlist-t2"><span class="vlist-r"><span class="vlist" style="height:0.2952em;"><span style="top:-2.55em;margin-left:0em;margin-right:0.05em;"><span class="pstrut" style="height:2.7em;"></span><span class="sizing reset-size6 size3 mtight"><span class="mord mtight"><span class="mrel mtight">≥</span><span class="mord mathnormal mtight">p</span></span></span></span></span><span class="vlist-s">​</span></span><span class="vlist-r"><span class="vlist" style="height:0.2861em;"><span></span></span></span></span></span></span></span></span></span>.</li>
<li><strong>LTL<span class="katex"><span class="katex-html" aria-hidden="true"><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height:1em;vertical-align:-0.25em;"></span><span class="mopen">[</span><span class="mord mathbb">F</span><span class="mclose">]</span></span></span></span></strong> with semiring-valued semantics for quantitative verification.</li>
