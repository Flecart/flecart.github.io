32:T3cc95,<h2 id="differential-privacy">Differential Privacy<a class="anchor" aria-hidden="true" hidden href="#differential-privacy">#</a></h2>
<h3 id="motivation-historical-context">Motivation &#x26; Historical Context<a class="anchor" aria-hidden="true" hidden href="#motivation-historical-context">#</a></h3>
<p>Privacy in statistical databases is the foundational problem: how to release useful aggregate information about a population without leaking information about individuals. Pre-DP approaches all failed under composition with auxiliary information.</p>
<h4 id="daleniuss-desideratum">Dalenius's Desideratum<a class="anchor" aria-hidden="true" hidden href="#daleniuss-desideratum">#</a></h4>
<blockquote>
<p>"Anything that can be learned about a respondent from the statistical database should be learnable without access to the database." ~ <em>Tore Dalenius, 1977</em></p>
</blockquote>
<p><strong>Impossibility result</strong> (Dwork 2006): This semantic notion is unachievable for any non-trivial database utility. The proof is essentially a <em>Bayesian</em> argument: if the database is informative, releasing it shifts posteriors over individuals, even for individuals not in the database. Example: a study showing "smoking causes cancer" reveals information about a smoker not in the study.</p>
<p>This forced the field to move from <strong>absolute privacy</strong> to <strong>relative privacy</strong> — i.e., the database's release should not significantly <em>increase</em> what an adversary learns about any individual <em>compared to a counterfactual world where that individual opted out</em>. This is the conceptual seed of DP.</p>
<div class="callout" data-callout="note">
<div class="callout-title"><span class="callout-icon" aria-hidden="true"><svg xmlns="http://www.w3.org/2000/svg" viewBox="0 0 24 24" width="18" height="18" fill="none" stroke="currentColor" stroke-width="2" stroke-linecap="round" stroke-linejoin="round"><path d="M12 20h9"></path><path d="M16.5 3.5a2.12 2.12 0 0 1 3 3L7 19l-4 1 1-4Z"></path></svg></span><span class="callout-title-inner">The opt-out semantics</span></div>
<div class="callout-content"><p>DP guarantees: your privacy loss is bounded by the marginal effect of <em>your</em> data being in the database. It does <strong>not</strong> guarantee that an adversary cannot learn things about you — only that they learn essentially the same things whether you participated or not.</p></div>
</div>
<h4 id="why-anonymization-fails">Why Anonymization Fails<a class="anchor" aria-hidden="true" hidden href="#why-anonymization-fails">#</a></h4>
<p><strong>k-anonymity</strong> (Samarati–Sweeney 1998): Each record indistinguishable from k−1 others on quasi-identifiers.
<strong>ℓ-diversity</strong> (Machanavajjhala 2007): Each equivalence class has ℓ "well-represented" sensitive values.
<strong>t-closeness</strong> (Li 2007): Distribution of sensitive attributes in each class close to global distribution.</p>
<p>All three fall to <strong>linkage attacks</strong> with auxiliary information:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>GIC Massachusetts (1997)</strong>: Latanya Sweeney re-identified Governor Weld from "anonymized" hospital records using voter rolls (ZIP + DOB + sex).</li>
<li><strong>AOL search log release (2006)</strong>: Searcher #4417749 → Thelma Arnold.</li>
<li><strong>Netflix Prize (2008)</strong>: Narayanan–Shmatikov linked Netflix ratings to IMDb profiles.</li>
<li><strong>Genome-wide association studies</strong>: Homer et al. (2008) detected presence in pooled allele frequencies.</li>
</ul>
<p>The deeper lesson: <strong>syntactic</strong> privacy guarantees (about the data format) cannot survive arbitrary auxiliary information; only <strong>semantic</strong> guarantees (about the mechanism) can.</p>

<h3 id="fundamental-properties">Fundamental Properties<a class="anchor" aria-hidden="true" hidden href="#fundamental-properties">#</a></h3>
<h4 id="post-processing-immunity">Post-Processing Immunity<a class="anchor" aria-hidden="true" hidden href="#post-processing-immunity">#</a></h4>
<blockquote>
<p>If <span class="katex"><span class="katex-html" aria-hidden="true"><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height:0.6833em;"></span><span class="mord mathcal">M</span></span></span></span> is (ε,δ)-DP and <span class="katex"><span class="katex-html" aria-hidden="true"><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height:0.8889em;vertical-align:-0.1944em;"></span><span class="mord mathnormal" style="margin-right:0.1076em;">f</span></span></span></span> is any (possibly randomized) function on <span class="katex"><span class="katex-html" aria-hidden="true"><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height:0.7805em;vertical-align:-0.0972em;"></span><span class="mord mathcal" style="margin-right:0.0822em;">Y</span></span></span></span>, then <span class="katex"><span class="katex-html" aria-hidden="true"><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height:0.8889em;vertical-align:-0.1944em;"></span><span class="mord mathnormal" style="margin-right:0.1076em;">f</span><span class="mspace" style="margin-right:0.2222em;"></span><span class="mbin">∘</span><span class="mspace" style="margin-right:0.2222em;"></span></span><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height:0.6833em;"></span><span class="mord mathcal">M</span></span></span></span> is (ε,δ)-DP.</p>
</blockquote>
<p>This is <em>the</em> most useful property in practice: once data is privatized, <strong>any downstream computation preserves the guarantee</strong>. No need to track privacy through ML training, plotting, summary tables, etc., as long as the original release was DP and no fresh data is touched.</p>
<p>Proof sketch: For any <span class="katex"><span class="katex-html" aria-hidden="true"><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height:0.6833em;"></span><span class="mord mathnormal" style="margin-right:0.0576em;">S</span></span></span></span>, <span class="katex"><span class="katex-html" aria-hidden="true"><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height:1.0641em;vertical-align:-0.25em;"></span><span class="mord"><span class="mord mathnormal" style="margin-right:0.1076em;">f</span><span class="msupsub"><span class="vlist-t"><span class="vlist-r"><span class="vlist" style="height:0.8141em;"><span style="top:-3.063em;margin-right:0.05em;"><span class="pstrut" style="height:2.7em;"></span><span class="sizing reset-size6 size3 mtight"><span class="mord mtight"><span class="mord mtight">−</span><span class="mord mtight">1</span></span></span></span></span></span></span></span></span><span class="mopen">(</span><span class="mord mathnormal" style="margin-right:0.0576em;">S</span><span class="mclose">)</span></span></span></span> is measurable, then apply DP definition to that set.</p>

<h4 id="randomized-response">Randomized Response<a class="anchor" aria-hidden="true" hidden href="#randomized-response">#</a></h4>
<p>The original DP mechanism (Warner 1965, predating the formalism by 40 years). For a yes/no question:</p>
<ul>
<li>With probability <span class="katex"><span class="katex-html" aria-hidden="true"><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height:0.7335em;vertical-align:-0.1944em;"></span><span class="mord mathnormal">p</span><span class="mspace" style="margin-right:0.2778em;"></span><span class="mrel">></span><span class="mspace" style="margin-right:0.2778em;"></span></span><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height:1em;vertical-align:-0.25em;"></span><span class="mord">1/2</span></span></span></span>, answer truthfully.</li>
<li>With probability <span class="katex"><span class="katex-html" aria-hidden="true"><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height:0.7278em;vertical-align:-0.0833em;"></span><span class="mord">1</span><span class="mspace" style="margin-right:0.2222em;"></span><span class="mbin">−</span><span class="mspace" style="margin-right:0.2222em;"></span></span><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height:0.625em;vertical-align:-0.1944em;"></span><span class="mord mathnormal">p</span></span></span></span>, flip a fair coin and answer.</li>
</ul>
<p>This is <span class="katex"><span class="katex-html" aria-hidden="true"><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height:1.2286em;vertical-align:-0.4811em;"></span><span class="mop">lo<span style="margin-right:0.0139em;">g</span></span><span class="mspace" style="margin-right:0.1667em;"></span><span class="mord"><span class="mopen nulldelimiter"></span><span class="mfrac"><span class="vlist-t vlist-t2"><span class="vlist-r"><span class="vlist" style="height:0.7475em;"><span style="top:-2.655em;"><span class="pstrut" style="height:3em;"></span><span class="sizing reset-size6 size3 mtight"><span class="mord mtight"><span class="mord mtight">1</span><span class="mbin mtight">−</span><span class="mord mathnormal mtight">p</span></span></span></span><span style="top:-3.23em;"><span class="pstrut" style="height:3em;"></span><span class="frac-line" style="border-bottom-width:0.04em;"></span></span><span style="top:-3.4461em;"><span class="pstrut" style="height:3em;"></span><span class="sizing reset-size6 size3 mtight"><span class="mord mtight"><span class="mord mathnormal mtight">p</span></span></span></span></span><span class="vlist-s">​</span></span><span class="vlist-r"><span class="vlist" style="height:0.4811em;"><span></span></span></span></span></span><span class="mclose nulldelimiter"></span></span></span></span></span>-DP at the individual level. <h4 id="sparse-vector-technique-svt">Sparse Vector Technique (SVT)<a class="anchor" aria-hidden="true" hidden href="#sparse-vector-technique-svt">#</a></h4>
<p>Answers a stream of threshold queries <span class="katex"><span class="katex-html" aria-hidden="true"><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height:1em;vertical-align:-0.25em;"></span><span class="mord"><span class="mord mathnormal" style="margin-right:0.1076em;">f</span><span class="msupsub"><span class="vlist-t vlist-t2"><span class="vlist-r"><span class="vlist" style="height:0.3117em;"><span style="top:-2.55em;margin-left:-0.1076em;margin-right:0.05em;"><span class="pstrut" style="height:2.7em;"></span><span class="sizing reset-size6 size3 mtight"><span class="mord mathnormal mtight">i</span></span></span></span><span class="vlist-s">​</span></span><span class="vlist-r"><span class="vlist" style="height:0.15em;"><span></span></span></span></span></span></span><span class="mopen">(</span><span class="mord mathnormal" style="margin-right:0.0278em;">D</span><span class="mclose">)</span><span class="mspace" style="margin-right:0.2778em;"></span><span class="mrel amsrm">≷</span><span class="mspace" style="margin-right:0.2778em;"></span></span><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height:0.6833em;"></span><span class="mord mathnormal" style="margin-right:0.1389em;">T</span></span></span></span> but only <strong>pays privacy for the YES answers</strong>.</p>
<p><strong>Algorithm</strong>: Add noise to the threshold <span class="katex"><span class="katex-html" aria-hidden="true"><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height:0.6833em;"></span><span class="mord mathnormal" style="margin-right:0.1389em;">T</span></span></span></span> once, then add fresh noise to each <span class="katex"><span class="katex-html" aria-hidden="true"><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height:1em;vertical-align:-0.25em;"></span><span class="mord"><span class="mord mathnormal" style="margin-right:0.1076em;">f</span><span class="msupsub"><span class="vlist-t vlist-t2"><span class="vlist-r"><span class="vlist" style="height:0.3117em;"><span style="top:-2.55em;margin-left:-0.1076em;margin-right:0.05em;"><span class="pstrut" style="height:2.7em;"></span><span class="sizing reset-size6 size3 mtight"><span class="mord mathnormal mtight">i</span></span></span></span><span class="vlist-s">​</span></span><span class="vlist-r"><span class="vlist" style="height:0.15em;"><span></span></span></span></span></span></span><span class="mopen">(</span><span class="mord mathnormal" style="margin-right:0.0278em;">D</span><span class="mclose">)</span></span></span></span>. Output "above" if noisy <span class="katex"><span class="katex-html" aria-hidden="true"><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height:0.8889em;vertical-align:-0.1944em;"></span><span class="mord"><span class="mord mathnormal" style="margin-right:0.1076em;">f</span><span class="msupsub"><span class="vlist-t vlist-t2"><span class="vlist-r"><span class="vlist" style="height:0.3117em;"><span style="top:-2.55em;margin-left:-0.1076em;margin-right:0.05em;"><span class="pstrut" style="height:2.7em;"></span><span class="sizing reset-size6 size3 mtight"><span class="mord mathnormal mtight">i</span></span></span></span><span class="vlist-s">​</span></span><span class="vlist-r"><span class="vlist" style="height:0.15em;"><span></span></span></span></span></span></span></span></span></span> exceeds noisy <span class="katex"><span class="katex-html" aria-hidden="true"><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height:0.6833em;"></span><span class="mord mathnormal" style="margin-right:0.1389em;">T</span></span></span></span>; stop after <span class="katex"><span class="katex-html" aria-hidden="true"><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height:0.4306em;"></span><span class="mord mathnormal">c</span></span></span></span> "above"s.</p>
<p><strong>Privacy</strong>: <span class="katex"><span class="katex-html" aria-hidden="true"><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height:1em;vertical-align:-0.25em;"></span><span class="mopen">(</span><span class="mord mathnormal">ε</span><span class="mpunct">,</span><span class="mspace" style="margin-right:0.1667em;"></span><span class="mord">0</span><span class="mclose">)</span></span></span></span>-DP with budget independent of the number of NO answers — pay only for <span class="katex"><span class="katex-html" aria-hidden="true"><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height:0.4306em;"></span><span class="mord mathnormal">c</span></span></span></span> above-threshold answers. <h3 id="dp-in-machine-learning">DP in Machine Learning<a class="anchor" aria-hidden="true" hidden href="#dp-in-machine-learning">#</a></h3>
<h4 id="dp-sgd">DP-SGD<a class="anchor" aria-hidden="true" hidden href="#dp-sgd">#</a></h4>
<p><strong>Abadi–Chu–Goodfellow–McMahan–Mironov–Talwar–Zhang 2016</strong>: The dominant approach to training DP models.</p>
<pre><code class="language-python">for batch in batches:
    grads = []
    for x in batch:
        g = compute_per_example_gradient(x)
        g = g * min(1, C / ||g||_2)   # per-example clipping
        grads.append(g)
    g_sum = sum(grads)
    g_sum = g_sum + N(0, sigma^2 * C^2 * I)   # Gaussian noise
    theta = theta - lr * g_sum / batch_size
</code></pre>
<p><strong>Key ingredients</strong>:</p>
<ol>
<li><strong>Per-example gradient clipping</strong> to bound <span class="katex"><span class="katex-html" aria-hidden="true"><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height:0.8333em;vertical-align:-0.15em;"></span><span class="mord"><span class="mord">Δ</span><span class="msupsub"><span class="vlist-t vlist-t2"><span class="vlist-r"><span class="vlist" style="height:0.3011em;"><span style="top:-2.55em;margin-left:0em;margin-right:0.05em;"><span class="pstrut" style="height:2.7em;"></span><span class="sizing reset-size6 size3 mtight"><span class="mord mtight">2</span></span></span></span><span class="vlist-s">​</span></span><span class="vlist-r"><span class="vlist" style="height:0.15em;"><span></span></span></span></span></span></span></span></span></span> to <span class="katex"><span class="katex-html" aria-hidden="true"><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height:0.6833em;"></span><span class="mord mathnormal" style="margin-right:0.0715em;">C</span></span></span></span></li>
<li><strong>Gaussian noise</strong> scaled to <span class="katex"><span class="katex-html" aria-hidden="true"><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height:0.6833em;"></span><span class="mord mathnormal" style="margin-right:0.0715em;">C</span></span></span></span> for <span class="katex"><span class="katex-html" aria-hidden="true"><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height:1em;vertical-align:-0.25em;"></span><span class="mopen">(</span><span class="mord mathnormal">ε</span><span class="mpunct">,</span><span class="mspace" style="margin-right:0.1667em;"></span><span class="mord mathnormal" style="margin-right:0.0379em;">δ</span><span class="mclose">)</span></span></span></span>-DP</li>
<li><strong>Privacy amplification by subsampling</strong>: a <span class="katex"><span class="katex-html" aria-hidden="true"><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height:1em;vertical-align:-0.25em;"></span><span class="mopen">(</span><span class="mord mathnormal">ε</span><span class="mpunct">,</span><span class="mspace" style="margin-right:0.1667em;"></span><span class="mord mathnormal" style="margin-right:0.0379em;">δ</span><span class="mclose">)</span></span></span></span>-DP mechanism applied to a <span class="katex"><span class="katex-html" aria-hidden="true"><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height:0.625em;vertical-align:-0.1944em;"></span><span class="mord mathnormal" style="margin-right:0.0359em;">q</span></span></span></span>-Poisson-subsample of <span class="katex"><span class="katex-html" aria-hidden="true"><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height:0.6833em;"></span><span class="mord mathnormal" style="margin-right:0.0278em;">D</span></span></span></span> is <span class="katex"><span class="katex-html" aria-hidden="true"><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height:1em;vertical-align:-0.25em;"></span><span class="mopen">(</span><span class="mord mathnormal" style="margin-right:0.0278em;">O</span><span class="mopen">(</span><span class="mord mathnormal" style="margin-right:0.0359em;">q</span><span class="mord mathnormal">ε</span><span class="mclose">)</span><span class="mpunct">,</span><span class="mspace" style="margin-right:0.1667em;"></span><span class="mord mathnormal" style="margin-right:0.0359em;">q</span><span class="mord mathnormal" style="margin-right:0.0379em;">δ</span><span class="mclose">)</span></span></span></span>-DP for small <span class="katex"><span class="katex-html" aria-hidden="true"><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height:0.4306em;"></span><span class="mord mathnormal">ε</span></span></span></span> (Beimel et al. 2014, Balle et al. 2018)</li>
<li><strong>RDP accounting</strong> across steps</li>
</ol>
<p><strong>Practical reality</strong>: Strong DP guarantees (<span class="katex"><span class="katex-html" aria-hidden="true"><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height:0.7719em;vertical-align:-0.136em;"></span><span class="mord mathnormal">ε</span><span class="mspace" style="margin-right:0.2778em;"></span><span class="mrel">≤</span><span class="mspace" style="margin-right:0.2778em;"></span></span><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height:0.6444em;"></span><span class="mord">1</span></span></span></span>) cost ~10-20% accuracy on ImageNet-scale tasks (De et al. 2022, Berrada et al. 2023). Recent work (Sander et al., Bu et al.) closes much of this gap via large batches, foundation-model fine-tuning, and architectural choices (group normalization beats batch normalization).</p>

<h4 id="pate">PATE<a class="anchor" aria-hidden="true" hidden href="#pate">#</a></h4>

<h4 id="distributed/federated-dp">Distributed/Federated DP<a class="anchor" aria-hidden="true" hidden href="#distributed/federated-dp">#</a></h4>
<p>Federated learning naturally pairs with DP to give two layers of protection. Two variants:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Central DP under FL</strong>: Server adds noise to aggregated gradients (still needs trusted server).</li>
<li><strong>Local DP per client</strong>: Each client adds noise; needs aggregation tricks (secure aggregation + DP) to recover utility.</li>
</ul>
<p>Combination with <strong>secure aggregation</strong> (cryptographic MPC) lets the server see only the noisy sum, getting central-DP utility with local-DP trust.</p>
<h3 id="dp-mechanism-design-game-theory">DP, Mechanism Design &#x26; Game Theory<a class="anchor" aria-hidden="true" hidden href="#dp-mechanism-design-game-theory">#</a></h3>

<div class="callout-content"><p>DP is mechanism design under a specific objective (privacy as a property of the mechanism), and the proof techniques migrate in both directions.</p></div>

<h4 id="joint-differential-privacy">Joint Differential Privacy<a class="anchor" aria-hidden="true" hidden href="#joint-differential-privacy">#</a></h4>
<p><strong>Kearns–Pai–Roth–Ullman 2014</strong>: Allows agent <span class="katex"><span class="katex-html" aria-hidden="true"><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height:0.6595em;"></span><span class="mord mathnormal">i</span></span></span></span>'s <strong>own output</strong> to depend strongly on their input (e.g., they receive their allocation), but the <strong>joint output to others</strong> is DP in <span class="katex"><span class="katex-html" aria-hidden="true"><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height:0.6595em;"></span><span class="mord mathnormal">i</span></span></span></span>. This is the right notion for matching markets, congestion games, and large-game equilibrium computation.</p>
<p><strong>Application</strong>: JDP mechanisms compute approximate correlated equilibria of large anonymous games. Connection point with your <strong>mechanism-design-for-AI-cooperation</strong> work: in multi-agent settings with strategic LLM agents, JDP might be the right notion of "private mechanism" since each agent observes its own action recommendation.</p>
<h4 id="mechanism-design-via-dp">Mechanism Design via DP<a class="anchor" aria-hidden="true" hidden href="#mechanism-design-via-dp">#</a></h4>
<p><strong>Nissim–Smorodinsky–Tennenholtz 2012</strong>: Use DP to compute approximate equilibria in <strong>digital goods auctions</strong>, <strong>matching with constraints</strong>, and <strong>facility location</strong> when exact truthful mechanisms don't exist.</p>
<p>The pattern: DP gives mechanisms that are robust to a single agent's misreport, which is essentially the definition of approximate strategyproofness. This trade — privacy for truthfulness — is a candidate framework for <strong>incentive-compatible AI agent protocols</strong> under your Cooperation Gap framework, where contractual incompleteness already forces approximate guarantees.</p>

<h3 id="practical-realities-critiques">Practical Realities &#x26; Critiques<a class="anchor" aria-hidden="true" hidden href="#practical-realities-critiques">#</a></h3>
<h4 id="what-does-ε-mean-in-practice">What does ε mean in practice?<a class="anchor" aria-hidden="true" hidden href="#what-does-ε-mean-in-practice">#</a></h4>
<p>ε has no universally agreed semantics. Common heuristics:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>ε ≤ 1</strong>: Strong privacy, recommended by Dwork.</li>
<li><strong>ε ∈ [1, 10]</strong>: "Moderate," used in DP-SGD ML papers.</li>
<li><strong>ε > 10</strong>: Effectively no meaningful pure-DP guarantee, though some hypothesis-testing interpretation remains.</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Apple's per-query ε of 4–8</strong>: Defensible only because of (a) bounded query rate and (b) the use of LDP, where one bad query cannot fully leak any user.</p>
<p><strong>Census 2020 PLB</strong>: Privacy Loss Budget per geographic level — different for total, P1, P2, … (P1 = sex by age, P2 = race, etc.) Total ε across all queries is small per person but compositional accounting is fragile.</p>
<h4 id="critiques">Critiques<a class="anchor" aria-hidden="true" hidden href="#critiques">#</a></h4>
<ul>
<li><strong>Tao Xu, Lin Liu et al.</strong>: DP guarantees rely on the worst-case adversary, but real adversaries may be weaker — DP overcorrects in some settings.</li>
<li><strong>Cynthia Dwork on δ</strong>: "δ is a probability of completely embarrassing failure" — needs to be cryptographically small.</li>
<li><strong>Kohli–Laskowski 2018</strong>: Survey participants don't understand ε; "differential privacy" becomes marketing.</li>
<li><strong>Bagdasaryan–Poursaeed–Shmatikov 2019</strong>: DP-SGD has <strong>disparate impact</strong> — minorities suffer larger accuracy loss because their data is more "atypical" and gets clipped/noised more.</li>
</ul>
<div class="callout" data-callout="question">
<div class="callout-title"><span class="callout-icon" aria-hidden="true"><svg xmlns="http://www.w3.org/2000/svg" viewBox="0 0 24 24" width="18" height="18" fill="none" stroke="currentColor" stroke-width="2" stroke-linecap="round" stroke-linejoin="round"><circle cx="12" cy="12" r="10"></circle><path d="M9.09 9a3 3 0 0 1 5.83 1c0 2-3 3-3 3"></path><path d="M12 17h.01"></path></svg></span><span class="callout-title-inner">Open question</span></div>
<div class="callout-content"><p>Is there a notion of <strong>group-fair</strong> DP that controls per-subgroup accuracy loss? Recent work by Yaghini, Mironov et al. on "individual privacy accounting" partially addresses this.</p></div>
</div>
<h4 id="drawbacks">Drawbacks<a class="anchor" aria-hidden="true" hidden href="#drawbacks">#</a></h4>
<ul>
<li>Worst-case sensitivity often dominates noise scale; smooth/local sensitivity helps but is harder to use.</li>
<li>Strong assumption: <strong>single-record sensitivity</strong> is the right unit. Households, time-series, social networks break this.</li>
<li>Hard to set ε from first principles (privacy budget allocation is an art).</li>
<li>Composition can quickly exhaust budget; once <span class="katex"><span class="katex-html" aria-hidden="true"><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height:0.4306em;"></span><span class="mord mathnormal">ε</span></span></span></span> is spent, you cannot <strong>re-query</strong>.</li>
</ul>
<h3 id="connections-further-reading">Connections &#x26; Further Reading<a class="anchor" aria-hidden="true" hidden href="#connections-further-reading">#</a></h3>
<ul>
<li>Robust Statistics: DP estimators are automatically robust to single-point contamination; explicit connection via the "propose-test-release" framework (Dwork–Lei 2009).</li>
<li>Stability and Generalization: DP <span class="katex"><span class="katex-html" aria-hidden="true"><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height:0.7719em;vertical-align:-0.136em;"></span><span class="mrel">⊆</span></span></span></span> "perfect stability" in the sense of Bousquet–Elisseeff.</li>
<li>Mechanism Design: Approximate truthfulness via exponential mechanism; JDP for large games.</li>
<li>Information Theory: Hypothesis-testing tradeoff function <span class="katex"><span class="katex-html" aria-hidden="true"><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height:0.8889em;vertical-align:-0.1944em;"></span><span class="mord mathnormal" style="margin-right:0.1076em;">f</span></span></span></span>-DP makes the connection to Le Cam–style le bounds explicit.</li>
<li>Cryptography: DP <span class="katex"><span class="katex-html" aria-hidden="true"><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height:0.8889em;vertical-align:-0.1944em;"></span><span class="mrel"><span class="mrel"><span class="mord vbox"><span class="thinbox"><span class="rlap"><span class="strut" style="height:0.8889em;vertical-align:-0.1944em;"></span><span class="inner"><span class="mord"><span class="mrel"></span></span></span><span class="fix"></span></span></span></span></span><span class="mspace nobreak"></span><span class="mrel">=</span></span></span></span></span> secure computation. <strong>MPC + DP</strong> gives strongest joint guarantee. The "shuffle model" is essentially a weak MPC primitive.</li>
<li>Algorithmic Fairness: Some tensions (disparate impact of DP-SGD) and some synergies (DP as a form of stability that helps generalize fairness constraints).</li>
<li>Pufferfish Privacy: The right generalization for <strong>correlated data</strong> (genomes, time series, networks).</li>
<li>Your <strong>Cooperation Gap</strong> framework: DP-style <span class="katex"><span class="katex-html" aria-hidden="true"><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height:0.4306em;"></span><span class="mord mathnormal">ε</span></span></span></span>-truthfulness may give a quantitative version of "how close can mechanisms get to substituting cooperation" under contractual incompleteness.</li>
