26:T3bb4,<h4 id="cosa-e">Cosa è<a class="anchor" aria-hidden="true" hidden href="#cosa-e">#</a></h4>
<p><strong>UML</strong> è un linguaggio di modelling (molto vecchio) ma ancora di continua evoluzione, da
un punto di vista storico è nato insieme ai concetti di <strong>Object Oriented Programming</strong> che ora è molto presente all'interno dell'industria, descritto bene in <a href="/notes/classi-oop/">Classi OOP</a>, anche se in questa occasione sviluppata in maniera molto più intuitiva (grafica).</p>
<h4 id="perche-serve">Perché serve<a class="anchor" aria-hidden="true" hidden href="#perche-serve">#</a></h4>
<p>Per cercare di <strong>comunicare</strong> quanto necessario riguardo struttura e dinamicità dell'architettura.</p>
<h3 id="struttura-di-uml">Struttura di UML<a class="anchor" aria-hidden="true" hidden href="#struttura-di-uml">#</a></h3>
<h4 id="structural-diagram">Structural Diagram<a class="anchor" aria-hidden="true" hidden href="#structural-diagram">#</a></h4>
<p>These diagrams focus on representing the <strong>static structure of a system</strong>. They help depict the components, classes, objects, and their relationships in a system. Some common structural diagrams in UML include:</p>
<ul>
<li>Class Diagram: Shows the classes in a system and their relationships, attributes, and methods.</li>
<li>Object Diagram: Represents instances of classes and their relationships at a specific point in time.</li>
<li>Component Diagram: Illustrates the physical components of a system and their dependencies.</li>
<li>Package Diagram: Organizes classes and other elements into packages to show their relationships.</li>
</ul>
<h4 id="behavioral-diagrams-4">Behavioral diagrams (4)<a class="anchor" aria-hidden="true" hidden href="#behavioral-diagrams-4">#</a></h4>
<p>These diagrams focus on illustrating the <strong>dynamic aspects of a system</strong>, including how it behaves and interacts over time. They are used to model the interactions between objects, the flow of control, and the system's behavior. Common behavioral diagrams in UML include:</p>
<ul>
<li>Use Case Diagram: Depicts the interactions between actors (users or external systems) and the system to achieve specific goals or functions.</li>
<li>Sequence Diagram: Shows the chronological sequence of messages exchanged between objects over time.</li>
<li>Statechart Diagram: Represents the various states an object or system can be in and how it transitions between those states.</li>
<li>Activity Diagram: Describes the flow of activities or processes within a system.</li>
</ul>
<h3 id="main-relationships">Main relationships<a class="anchor" aria-hidden="true" hidden href="#main-relationships">#</a></h3>
<p>Queste sono anche chiamate le freccie, ossia le <strong>tipologie di relazioni</strong> che possono esistere fra entità diverse.</p>
<h4 id="le-relazioni-esistenti">Le relazioni esistenti<a class="anchor" aria-hidden="true" hidden href="#le-relazioni-esistenti">#</a></h4>
<ol>
<li>
<p><strong>Association:</strong> An association is a basic relationship that represents a link between two or more classes or objects. It indicates that instances of one class are related to instances of another class. In a graph context, you can think of associations as edges connecting nodes in a graph. Associations can have multiplicities to specify how many instances are involved in the relationship (e.g., one-to-one, one-to-many).</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Aggregation:</strong> Aggregation is a specialized form of association that represents a <em>whole-part relationship</em>. It indicates that one class (the whole) is composed of or contains instances of another class (the part). In a graph, aggregation can be seen as a hierarchical relationship, where nodes at one level represent composite objects made up of nodes at another level.
Esempio di aggregazione:
<img src="/images/notes/Unified Modeling Language-1696933376777.jpeg" style="width: 100%" class="center" alt="Unified Modeling Language-1696933376777"></p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Composition:</strong> Composition is a stronger form of aggregation, indicating a strict ownership relationship. It means that the whole class has exclusive responsibility for the existence and lifetime of its parts. In a graph, composition is similar to aggregation but with a stronger emphasis on the containment of parts within the whole.<img src="/images/notes/Unified Modeling Language-1696933472558.jpeg" style="width: 100%" class="center" alt="Unified Modeling Language-1696933472558"></p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Inheritance (Generalization):</strong> Inheritance, represented by a <em>solid arrow with an open triangle</em>, signifies an "is-a" relationship. It is used to model the inheritance hierarchy in object-oriented programming, where one class (the subclass or derived class) inherits attributes and methods from another class (the superclass or base class). In graph terms, it represents a <strong>hierarchy</strong>, with edges pointing from subclasses to their superclass.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Realization (Interface Implementation):</strong> Realization is used to show that a class or component implements a specific interface or fulfills a particular contract. It indicates a relationship between a classifier and an interface. In graph theory, this can be seen as a form of dependency or connection between nodes representing classes and interfaces.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Dependency:</strong> Dependency is a relationship that indicates that <em>one element relies on another element</em>. It can be used to represent various forms of relationships, such as method dependencies, parameter dependencies, or simple associations between classes. In a graph, dependencies are akin to edges indicating connections or reliance between nodes.</p>
</li>
</ol>
<h2 id="structural-diagrams">Structural diagrams<a class="anchor" aria-hidden="true" hidden href="#structural-diagrams">#</a></h2>
<h3 id="class-diagrams">Class diagrams<a class="anchor" aria-hidden="true" hidden href="#class-diagrams">#</a></h3>
<p>Descrivo in che modo le varia classi sono relazionati fra di loro (ad esempi con l'albero di ereditarietà)</p>
<h2 id="types-of-behavioral-diagrams-5">Types of behavioral diagrams (5)<a class="anchor" aria-hidden="true" hidden href="#types-of-behavioral-diagrams-5">#</a></h2>
<h3 id="use-cases">Use cases (!)<a class="anchor" aria-hidden="true" hidden href="#use-cases">#</a></h3>
<h4 id="structure-of-use-case-diagrams">Structure of use case diagrams<a class="anchor" aria-hidden="true" hidden href="#structure-of-use-case-diagrams">#</a></h4>
<blockquote>
<p>A use case is a concept in software engineering and system design that describes a specific <strong>interaction or set of interactions</strong> between a <em>system</em> (usually software) and its <em>external actors</em></p>
</blockquote>
<ol>
<li><strong>Goal-Oriented:</strong> Use cases are centered around achieving specific goals or objectives. Each use case represents a particular task, process, or scenario that an external actor wants to accomplish using the system.</li>
<li><strong>Actor:</strong> An actor is any external entity that interacts with the system. Actors can be users, other software systems, hardware devices, or any external entity that initiates a use case. Actors are defined based on their roles and responsibilities in the system.</li>
<li><strong>Flow of Events:</strong> A use case typically includes a description of the <em>main flow of events</em>, which outlines the steps or interactions involved in achieving the desired goal. It can also include alternative or exceptional flows to cover various scenarios.</li>
